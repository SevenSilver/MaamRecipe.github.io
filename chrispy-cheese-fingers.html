Chrispy Cheese Fingers

Cheese has many culinary and cultural significance worldwide. Cheese lovers from all age groups enjoy eating cheese-based foods such as cheese puffs, cheese sticks and cheese fingers. Cheese lovers may also refer to their favorite type of cheese as 'cheese fingers.' Eating chewy edibles coated in a layer of cheese whets the appetite and satisfies cravings. Essentially, eating cheese makes you feel gratifying and full.

Cheese is made by adding bacteria to milk, letting it culture for several hours and then removing the curded curd. The process creates a substance known as 'cheese curds.' The cheese maker then uses an apparatus known as a 'cheese press' to create a layer of cheese on top of the curd. This allows the eater to enjoy both the savory flavor and the tangy texture created by the addition of bacteria to the curd. Cheese devotees have different opinions regarding which type of curd makes the best cheese. However, characteristics such as mouthfeel and flavor are consistently agreed upon by experts. Some believe that goat or sheep cheeses have a richer flavor than cow cheeses due to their higher fat content. Regardless of which type of curd a person prefers, all cheesemakers follow similar processes when making their favorite food staple.

Cheese fingers are finger-sized snacks made from processed cheese. They're popular in the United States and other countries around the world. However, many people have their own opinion on what flavor of cheese should be used in the creation of a cheese finger. Popular cheese options include cheddar, Swiss and mozzarella. These additions bring a new dimension to traditional cheese fingers by adding a new flavor to the snack.

Popular cheese selections include cheddar, swiss and mozzarella. Each selection adds a unique flavor to traditional cheese fingers. Cheddar offers a strong, sharp taste perfect for complementing other flavors in a dish. Swiss has a sweet flavor that is similar to honey when it melts. Mozzarella has a mild flavor that is best appreciated when paired with sweet or savory dipping sauces. These selections are just a few of the many that can be used to create new flavor combinations for cheese fingers.

Many people like to eat cheese as an appetizer before meals. This way the diner can get their fill of cheese before eating any other food items. Traditional cheese fingers have cheddar as their primary cheese option. Cheddar is milder than some other cheeses which work well as an appet panic for most diners. However, many diners also like their cheeses 'sharp' when eating them as snacks. This motivates the creation of 'cheese fingers with a kick'. These have the same base ingredients, but add an extra level of flavor by using swiss instead of cheddar. This way the diner can still get their daily dose of cheddar while also enjoying a new flavor combination with their snack food fixation!

Lets start our dish.

Ingredients

2 tbsp ketchup

splash toasted sesame oil

1 tbsp soy sauce

1/2 cup all purpose flour

1/2 cup water

salt to taste

3/4 cups panko

350g extra firm cheese

Direction

Step 1

Make the quick katsu sauce by mixing together the ketchup, toasted sesame oil, and soy sauce.

Step 2

Mix together the flour, water, and a pinch of salt in a bowl.

Step 3

Pour the panko into a separate bowl and season with some salt.

Step 4

Carefully slice the extra firm cheese into sticks (fingers).

Step 5

Roll the cheese in the flour mixture. Use a fork to transfer the cheese onto the panko and roll the cheese to coat it.

Step 6

Set the cheese aside and repeat with the rest of the cheese sticks.

Step 7

Heat up a frying pan to medium heat. Add in the avocado oil and let is heat up for a few minutes. Stick a bamboo chopstick in the oil to see if it's ready, there will be small bubbles when it is.

Step 8

Carefully fry the cheese in small batches for about 45 seconds on each side.

Step 9

Set the cheese onto a paper towel to rest and absorb the excess oil.

Step 10

Serve the cheese katsu fingers with some shredded cabbage, fresh tomatoes, and that delicious katsu sauce.
