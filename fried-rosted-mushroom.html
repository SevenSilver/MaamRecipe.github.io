Fried Rosted Mushroom

Roasted mushrooms are a delicious dish prepared by frying mushrooms in butter or oil. Each variety of mushroom has its own unique flavor, making this dish a favorite for many people.

Fried
Emily Carey

07 Oct, 2022 3:00 pm

Mushrooms are good sources of protein, vitamins, minerals and dietary fiber. They contain many types of vitamins - especially C, K, B2, B6, B12 and niacin - along with various minerals like potassium, phosphorus, chromium, zinc and copper. Dietary fiber helps you feel full after eating food which is why some health experts recommend eating more dietary fiber for good health. People include mushrooms into dishes like pasta sauces to add flavor, but to also increase their nutritional value.

Roasted mushrooms are a favorite dish among vegetarians and meat eaters alike. They're a cheap, healthy food that tastes great when prepared well. Some of the best ways to prepare fried roasted mushrooms involve using the right spices and herbs. For instance, a combination of garlic, black pepper and sage yields fantastic flavor in just minutes. Plus, mushrooms cook quickly so you'll need to plan your cooking time ahead of time.

Fried mushrooms are easy to prepare and can be enjoyed any time of day. They taste great as a snack with bread or as an addition to other dishes. Served with a side of seasoned rice or mashed potatoes, roasted mushroom dishes make for an excellent mealtime experience. Furthermore, you can spice up your dinner by adding them to omelet recipes or as an ingredient in pasta sauces and soups. The flavors they add are delectable and universally pleasing.

Different types of fresh white mushrooms sell for more on grocery store shelves than brown varieties. However, most cooks agree that the white variety tastes sweeter than its brown counterpart. To get superior results when cooking with white mushrooms, you'll need to follow the instructions closely but supplement them with your own fresh herbs and spices. To enhance the sweet flavor of white mushrooms without sacrificing texture, include minced lemon zest, minced fresh thyme, sea salt and pepper along with the marinade ingredients when preparing the dish.

Fried roasting is one of the best cooking methods for preparing delicious white mushroom dishes. The savory taste is enhanced by various spices added during preparation- and can easily fill you up as a meal supplement or appetizer choice. No special equipment needed! Simply follow this easy recipe to try!

So without wasting anymore time let's get started!

Ingredients

150g oyster mushrooms

1 1/2 cup flour

3/4 cups almond milk

1/2 tbsp apple cider vinegar

2 tsp salt

pepper to taste

1/2 tsp oregano

1 tsp onion powder

1 tsp garlic powder

1 tsp smoked paprika

1/2 tsp cumin

1/4 tsp cinnamon

1/4 cup chickpea mayo

1-2 tbsp sriracha

2 cups avocado oil

few sprigs parsley

lemon wedges to serve

Direction

Step 1

Set up your work station with 2 plates and add 1 cup of flour onto one of the plates. Stir the apple cider vinegar into the almond milk and let it sit for a couple of minutes.

Step 2

Add 1/2 cup flour to another plate, season with salt, and pour in the almond milk. Whisk to dissolve the flour. Then, add a generous pinch of salt to the other plate followed by some pepper, oregano, onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, and cinnamon. Mix to combine.

Step 3

Coat the oyster mushrooms in the dry mix, then in the wet mix, and again in the dry mix (replenish the flour or almond milk as needed). Repeat until all of the oyster mushrooms are coated.

Step 4

Make the dipping sauce by mixing together the chickpea mayo and sriracha.

Step 5

Pour the avocado oil into a frying pan and heat up on medium heat for 2-3min. Stick a bamboo chopstick into the oil, if there are a lot of fast moving bubbles, it's ready.

Step 6

Carefully place in the oyster mushrooms. Fry in small batches to prevent overcrowding the pan. Cook for 3-4min. Flip the mushrooms over and cook for another couple of minutes.

Step 7

Carefully transfer the fried mushrooms onto a cooling rack and let them rest for a minute or so before serving.

Step 8

Serve with a sprinkle of salt, chopped parsley, and some lemon wedges.
