Mexican Tacos Recipe

Tacos are a staple in many Mexican diets. They're typically made from corn flour or wheat flour, but can also be made from protein or vegetables. Many people love tacos because they're delicious and nutritious.

Recipe Chili
Emily Carey

03 Oct, 2022 5:00 pm

People from Mexico and other Latin American countries make tacos out of whatever they have at home. This is because tacos are traditionally a homemade dish. Eating tacos at a restaurant isn't as good as eating them at home. That's because restaurants typically use better ingredients than what people have at home. People have many options when making tacos at home. They can use ground beef, chicken, pork, roasted potatoes and more. You can even add different types of sauces to create unique flavors. There are many ways to prepare a delicious meal that you'll enjoy making.

A taco is a traditional Mexican food that comes from Mexico's Central region. People in Mexico call this region 'La Centroavesta.' It's where the word 'taco' originated from. Today, many people enjoy making their own traditional tacos using ground meat, vegetables and other ingredients. They can also add sauces to give the dish flavor and make it more delicious. Making your own tacos is a great way to ensure they're both healthy and delicious. It's also a great way to add extra food to your meal without adding extra weight to your body. Restaurants typically don't have to worry about making their food taste good or safe for consumption. This can lead to them serving food that's unsafe for consumers. Tacos are one of the best foods for creating a healthy lifestyle for you and your loved ones.

There are many ways to make tacos that are both healthy and delicious- even without any recipes. Many people like to use ground beef as their primary meat for making tacos. However, you can use any type of ground meat you want- even pork or chicken if you prefer. You can also add various vegetables such as onion, tomato, corn and more to your taco filling mix. You can also add spices such as cumin, chili powder, oregano and more to spice up your taco mix. You can also add cheese such as cheddar or queso fresco to your taco mixture if you want it greasy or cheesy- flavored respectively. Adding melted cheese before adding the meat creates a great cheesy texture when biting into your homemade taco meal. You can also add different sauces such as salsa, guacamole and sour cream to your tacos for extra flavor and texture options. Adding tomato sauce before putting the ingredients in the pan helps it absorb into the meat better and adds extra flavor options for you to choose from when creating your dish.

Tacos are an excellent meal choice for anyone who wants to experiment with cooking or creating new meals based on their taste buds' preferences! The choices in creating new dishes based on what's available at home are endless thanks to the ingredients and spices available in most kitchens! The end product is a hearty meal that will satisfy any appetite!

Ingredients

1 carrot

90g lo bak (or daikon)

1/3 cup rice vinegar

2 + 2 tbsp maple syrup

1 tbsp soy sauce

1 large red onion

200g oyster mushrooms

1/4 cup plantbased mayo

2 tbsp sriracha

6 tortilla

2 tbsp chili oil

2 tbsp avocado oil

1 tbsp soy sauce

1 tsp dark soy sauce

few sprigs cilantro

sprinkle of white sesame seeds

Direction

Step 1

Slice the carrot and lo bak into thin matchsticks. Place them together in a mixing bowl and add the rice vinegar, 2 tbsp maple syrup, and soy sauce. Mix well and let it pickle in the fridge for a few hours or overnight.

Step 2

Thinly slice the red onion and roughly chop the oyster mushrooms .

Step 3

Combine the plantbased mayo with the sriracha.

Step 4

Place the tortilla onto a baking tray and preheat the oven to 350F .

Step 5

Heat up a nonstick pan to medium heat. Add the chili oil followed by the red onions. Sauté for 7-8min. Then, season with some salt and add 2 tbsp maple syrup. Sauté for another 2min. Set aside the caramelized onions.

Step 6

Heat the pan back to medium heat and add the avocado oil followed by the oyster mushrooms. Sauté for 7-8min. Then, add the soy sauce, dark soy sauce. Sauté for 2min and set aside.

Step 7

Heat up the tortilla in the oven for a few minutes.

Step 8

Plate and assemble the tacos with the mushrooms, red onions, pickled carrots, fresh cilantro, spicy mayo, and a sprinkle of white sesame seeds.
