Easy Tasty Noodles

Noodles are a dish commonly made from wheat flour and water. They are usually served as a main course with a variety of sauces, such as tomato sauce, chili sauce, and curry sauce. However, not all kinds of noodles are the same. Some are thick, while others are thin. Noodles can be dried, fresh or frozen and come in a variety of shapes and sizes. In addition, different types of noodles are used in different cultures- for example, Chinese noodles are used for soup in China and in Indian cuisine.

The earliest noodles were made from semmental flax- an agricultural product from the papyrus plant. In ancient Egypt and Greece, this was the standard when making pasta. At that time, water was used to make the pasta; it was then put into sand to dry to make it even more firm and durable. Pastas used in ancient Rome were made from wheat flour with a little bit of honey to give it a sweet taste. These pastas were used for savory dishes instead of desserts as they were not sweet enough for that purpose.

In the late 19th century, Josephcell Carpinetto introduced shop-bought pasta to Italy. He initially intended to make macaroni but realized he did not have the required skills to make it. Instead, he decided to import pre-made pasta from his native country. His product became a huge hit with Italian consumers which led to him being awarded the 'Padre Nostra Gold Medal' for his product quality. Over time, other convenience food manufacturers have made it easier for consumers to make noodles at home with their machines. They have many different kinds of machines designed for this purpose- including ones that can knead the dough and ones that can cut the noodles.

Now most people think that making their own noodles is difficult; they feel they do not know how to do it or what is involved in doing so. However, it is actually very easy- all you need is wheat flour, water and a pasta machine. To begin, you need to mix your ingredients together well in a bowl or container- this will ensure the pasta comes out nice and firm every time you make it. Next, use your machine to roll out your dough into thin sheets using specific attachments or rings on the machine's rotational axis. After that, cut your sheets into desired lengths using specific cutters or scissors depending on what you want your finished product to look like. Finally, boil your noodles in a large pot of water for 3-4 minutes before serving them up hot with your choice of sauce!

Noodles come in various varieties such as egg noodles, angel hair pasta and ziti pasta among others. They can be served dry or with sauces such as tomato sauce or curry sauce. Consumers can easily make fresh noodles at home with the help of convenience food manufacturers.

Ingredients

50g Snow Peas

1 Small Carrot

1/2 Red Bell Pepper

75g Extra Firm Tofu

1-2 Sticks Green Onion

2 Pieces Garlic

3 Rehydrated Shiitake Mushrooms

75g Frozen Udon Noodles

1 Tbsp Soy Sauce

1 Tsp Dark Soy Sauce

1 Tsp Rice Vinegar

Splash of Toasted Sesame Oil

2 Tbsp Ketchup

2 Tbsp Chili Oil

60g Baby Corn

60g Bean Sprouts

1/2 Tsp White Sesame Seeds

Direction

Step 1

Put water to boil in a pot for noodles.

Step 2

Peel and remove the snow peas. Cut small carrots into thin matchsticks. Chop the red bell pepper into bite sized pieces.

Step 3

Pat extra firm tofu dry with paper towels. Then, tear the tofu into small pieces. Keep the white and green parts of the green onion separately and chop them finely. thinly slice the garlic.

Step 4

Carefully remove the stalks from the rehydrated shiitake mushrooms and chop them.

Step 5

Boil udon noodles for half the time (in this case 3 minutes) according to package directions. Gently give them a stir to keep them from sticking.

Step 6

Make the stir fry sauce by combining soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice vinegar, toasted sesame oil and ketchup. Filter the udon noodles and set them aside.

Step 7

Make the stir fry sauce by combining soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice vinegar, toasted sesame oil and ketchup. Filter the udon noodles and set them aside.

Step 8

Heat up a nonstick pan to medium heat. Add the chili oil followed by the tofu and sauté for 3-4min.

Step 9

Add the shiitake mushrooms, snow peas, red bell peppers, and the baby corn. Sauté for 3-4 minutes.

Step 10

Add the shiitake mushrooms, snow peas, red bell peppers, and the baby corn. Sauté for 3-4 minutes.

Step 11

Add noodles and then add stir fry sauce. Sauté for another couple of minutes.

Step 12

Plate and garnish with the remaining green onions and white sesame seeds.
