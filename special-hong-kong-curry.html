Special Hong Kong Curry

Curry is a popular HK dish made from spices, meat, and vegetables cooked in a sauce. It originated in the Middle East millennia ago and is now popular all over the world. Curry is typically a dish of social significance, as it is typically served at religious ceremonies, festivals and other social occasions. Served with warm bread, curries are a delicious addition to any meal.

Ingredients

3 Pieces Garlic

Small Piece Ginger

1 Onion

1lb Russet Potatoes

170g Baby King Oyster Mushrooms

2 Sticks Green Onion

2 Tbsp Avocado Oil

1 Tsp Turmeric

1 Tbsp Curry Powder

2 Tbsp Chu Hou Paste

400ml Canned Coconut Milk

1/2 Cup Water

1 Tbsp Soy Sauce

1 Tbsp Cane Sugar

Few Sprigs Cilantro

Steamed Rice To Serve

Direction

Step 1

Finely chop the garlic and ginger. Dice the onion. Then, peel the potatoes and chop them into small cubes.

Step 2

Chop the baby king oyster mushrooms into bite sized medallions. Chop the green onions into small batons.

Step 3

Heat up a saut√© pan to medium heat. Add the avocado oil followed by the mushrooms. Saut√© for 4-5min. Give the mushrooms some time to sear and get some nice colour. Then, set them aside.

Step 4

Bring the pan back to medium heat. Add the onions and saut√© for 3-4 minutes.

Step 5

Add the garlic and ginger. Saut√© for 1 minute. Add the turmeric and curry powder. Saut√© for another minute and make a well in the middle of the pan.

Step 6

Add the chu hou paste and saut√© for 1-2min. Add the potatoes, mushrooms, coconut milk, and 1/2 cup water.

Step 7

Add the soy sauce, cane sugar and green onions. Give it a good stir and bring to a boil. Then, simmer on medium low for 15-20 minute.

Step 8

Plate the curry and garnish with some freshly chopped cilantro. Serve this with freshly steamed rice.
